Structure

Education in Design and Visual Communication. Our school, like all the othersin Italy, operates within a system defined by rules that dictate the relationship between teachers and students. However, recently ISIA U has launchedan experimental project to rethink the design teaching, creating an informallearning space self-managed by the students. This space is called Rumore.

Intention

Placing ourselves in the educational context as design students, we are reconsidering the methods by which our learning takes place. Drawing upon Illich’s theory of deschooling society, we believe that every new experience, lived directly in the field, is capable of generating a moment of learning to complement our educational path. In this sense, each of our perspectives and subjectivities becomes a resource capable of sparking new discoveries, when a process of collective analysis and learning is set in motion. We chose to concentrate our activity in a delimited space such as a table in order to break away from Rumore. We believe that our proposal is strengthened by the lateral responses that a new context can trigger. Crucial to our approach was the work of Lucius Burkhardt, a sociologist who made strolling a practice of reappropriation of places, also from a conceptual point of view. In 1993 in Kessel, he organized “The ZEBRAcrossing,” which allowed participants to cross a six-lane highway wherever they decided to lay down the striped carpet. The proposed activity gave back to the residents the possibility to cross the city at any point they desired. For Burkhardt, the context is nothing but a field of consciousness in which, thanks to interdisciplinary analysis tools, one is able to confront the visible and invisible aspects that constitute it. In this sense, the unpredictability of the generative context only underscores its potential. In addition, referring to experiences such as Pedro Rays’ Sanatorium and Conditional Design by Luna Maurer, Edo Paulus, Jonathan Puckey, and Roel Wouters, led us to formulate our proposal according to a structured and programmed methodology. Furthermore, fundamental to our reasoning was Macguffin - The Life of Things, a Dutch editorial research project that investigates the life of ordinary things, the unexplored possibilities that objects hold in relation to history identity, and social relations. As in the experience of Macguffin, the object becomes the expedient to go beyond.

Objective

We offer a conceptual field: our brackets. We offer a physical counterpart: the table. In this area, we have only one task: to redefine the starting object, by continuously defining it. Our sign, [...], which becomes the name and identity, carries with it part of the process we want to initiate. The ambiguity that arises in pronouncing it already hints at the interpretive possibilities contained within a sign. These multiple readings are the ones we want to investigate through the activity we propose. Proposing a laboratory activity such as [...] means exploring the relationship between object and knowledge; its success depends entirely on the willingness of individuals to take part in the process. In exchange for a concrete contribution, those who take part in this becoming will acquire a new way of knowing the object. Therefore, for us, the endpoint is the varied set of languages, thoughts, and perspectives that, starting from the object, also is capable of grasping its more lateral aspects. [...] is validated when another reality simultaneously initiates the same proposal. However, these two ones are not bound to make appointments at the same time, but only to share considerations within the defined twinnin time frame. The conclusion of the two proposals should occur in synchrony, so that there is a synthesis of both experiences. Every reality that engages in this activity is invited to contact another at the end of the project.

Work method

The collective work that the table proposes invites participants to develop discussions on identity starting from a fetish object. We call for a plural participation around the surface of the table to allow constant contamination, where the individual readings should be materialized. We confide in the ability of an object to convey knowledge; we believe that this potential can only be unlocked thanks to the subjectivity and cultural identity of each individual. The collective perspective, in this sense, is able to bring forth semantic bridges that could not arise otherwise. The reasoning generated from the object will be influenced by aspects such as the personal heritage of the individual, the role of the context and moment, social barriers, expectations of change, rooting, and relationships. Being [...] an activity that is determined by the spontaneous adhesion of the participants, only the first theme emerges from the project group. The subsequent appointments will originate from the latest contribution produced during the day. All that will be produced from the work done around the table will be tracked in a book-object that captures the controversial identity nature of a place; this editorial will consist of the outputs produced, the activity documentations, and the memorandum drawn up at the end of each event. The book will be a narrative and documentary product returned in an artistic and experimental key that offers an informal and unconventional memoir, capable of bringing out the hidden aspects that the official narrative usually cannot achieve. All stemming from the individual analysis of the starting theme.

Process

[...] is a workshop proposal that extends over several sessions. The first step is to define the venue where this activity can take place, taking into consideration their cultural relevance (libraries, theatres, museums, schools ...). Once permission has been granted by the location in the area of interest, the notification of the date, time, and position must be transmitted through one’s communication channels (a template will be provided with the necessary details and design system for each event). The communication will include the theme to be addressed. Upon arrival at the designated location, set up the table and position the necessary tools in the designated areas. Welcome participants by introducing the project, read the manifesto, and explain the objectives. Welcome sessions will be held every hour. The activity begins. Participants create contributions independently using the given tools. Record each completed contribution, keeping track of time data through scanning. Upload the scanned results into the online platform. Repeat the welcoming, production, recording, and uploading phases till the end of the activity. Conclusion of the activity: discovery of the next theme. Draw up a logbook documenting the pros and cons encountered during the proposal. Make the necessary modifications to your methodology to overcome the challenges faced. After all the scheduled sessions have been completed, draft an editorial project that serves as a documentative and educational tool.
